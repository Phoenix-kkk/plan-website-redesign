# SEO and GEO requirements

Use only the parts relevant to the redesign scope. Verify current platform-specific guidance with authoritative sources when it could have changed.

## Technical discovery baseline

Specify, as applicable:

- crawlable, indexable HTML for intended public pages;
- correct status codes, canonicals, redirects, and error behavior;
- a sitemap containing canonical indexable URLs with truthful modification signals;
- robots directives consistent with the intended access policy;
- stable, descriptive URLs and migration mappings;
- unique titles, visible primary headings, and useful descriptions;
- semantic HTML, accessible names, and descriptive media alternatives;
- structured data that matches visible content and the page's real entity type;
- internal links that make important pages discoverable without relying on client-side interactions;
- performance requirements tied to an agreed measurement method and environment.

Do not prescribe a crawler allowlist by default. Access policy is a business and legal decision, and crawler names and behavior can change.

## Intent and page mapping

Assign one primary search or discovery intent to each indexable page. Record supporting topics without creating overlapping pages for trivial keyword variants. Distinguish branded, category, problem, comparison, use-case, and informational intent where relevant.

## Citation-ready content

For pages expected to support AI-assisted discovery, require useful visible content rather than hidden markup alone:

- answer the page's core question early and plainly;
- define entities and relationships unambiguously;
- support important claims with attributable evidence;
- expose authorship, ownership, relevant dates, and update responsibility;
- use descriptive headings, tables, lists, and concise factual blocks when they improve comprehension;
- link to primary evidence and related first-party material;
- keep structured data consistent with visible content.

Treat these as clarity and credibility practices, not a guarantee of inclusion or citation by an AI system.

## Structured data

Choose types from the page's actual visible content and entity. Define required fields, data source, rendering location, and validation method. Do not add FAQ, review, rating, organization, product, or author markup unless the corresponding content and entity claims are present and supportable.

## Migration requirements

When URLs, domains, rendering systems, or information architecture change, include:

- old-to-new URL mapping and redirect ownership;
- canonical, sitemap, internal-link, hreflang, and analytics updates;
- handling for removed, merged, gated, or noindex content;
- prelaunch crawl and postlaunch monitoring;
- rollback criteria and responsible owners for high-risk migrations.

## Measurement

Separate leading indicators from outcomes:

- launch correctness: indexability, status codes, metadata, schema validity, redirects;
- discovery signals: impressions, indexed pages, query coverage, referral sources;
- business outcomes: qualified conversions, assisted conversions, revenue or pipeline where available;
- AI visibility observations: attributed mentions, cited sources, answer coverage, and repeatable test prompts.

Document the data source, baseline window, comparison window, and known limitations. Never fabricate a baseline or target.
